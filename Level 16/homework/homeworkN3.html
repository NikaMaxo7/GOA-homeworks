Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was one of the greatest inventors, artists, scientists, and engineers of the Italian Renaissance.
His most famous works are the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, which are irreplaceable in the history of art.
Da Vinci created sketches of flying machines, tanks, anatomical drawings, and many other inventions,
which are considered the forerunners of modern science and technology.
He studied the human body in detail and created accurate anatomical drawings that are still used today.
Many of his ideas were unprepared for practical use at the time,
but later technological progress made them possible.
Da Vinci left behind numerous cryptic notes in which he studied science, art, and engineering.
He is considered a universal genius whose ideas were centuries ahead of his time.
