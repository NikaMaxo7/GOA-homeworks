Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa (1503-1506) is Leonardo da Vinci's most famous and one of the most recognizable paintings in the world.
It depicts the enigmatic smile of an unidentified woman, which is considered a special masterpiece in the history of painting.
The painting is made with oil paints on wood and is distinguished by carefully processed shadows and soft transitions (sfumato technique).
The Mona Lisa is kept in the Louvre Museum in Paris, and is one of the most protected and admired works of art.
